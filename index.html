Investor FAQ

The straight answers.

SPCTR builds the tools gaming creators and agencies actually need, and uses the data those tools generate to set the price for the category. We are raising $400K. Here are the questions investors ask first, answered plainly.

Updated May 22, 2026
Q1
What is SPCTR?
SPCTR builds the tools gaming creators and agencies actually need, and uses the data those tools generate to set the price for the category. Generic creator-marketing software wasn't built for gaming, so creators and agencies have been working without the right data and brand deals get priced on guesses. The brand side is just as blind: there are hundreds of games, thousands of niche creators inside them, and nobody has translated what each of them is worth or why a brand should care. SPCTR builds these tools for gaming creators and their agencies. The data those tools produce makes every deal more profitable, and gives the whole category a real price the creator side trusts and the brand side can understand. The software around the pricing runs the agency: the creator roster with live cross-platform analytics, campaigns broken into briefs, brief assignment, and a deal pipeline. Each creator gets a command center: a content calendar and daily briefing, their assigned briefs, cross-platform analytics, an AI Coach, and an auto-built media kit.
Q2
Who is it for, and why sell to agencies?
Gaming creator agencies and the creators on their rosters. The agency is the paying customer; creators come onto the platform under their agency. One agency signature puts a whole roster on the platform at once. The agency holds the budget and the roster comes with the deal, which is far more efficient than signing creators one at a time.
Q3
Do you have customers?
Yes. Amplified IM, a mobile-gaming creator agency with a 75-plus creator roster, is signed on a 12-month agreement (auto-renews) and launches on the platform June 15, 2026. As the founding customer, Amplified is on a $1,000/month founding rate, locked for life while the agreement remains in continuous effect, roughly $11K in year one. A future rate change would require a signed addendum from both parties. The creator-facing side of the platform was hardened through a closed pilot with 15 of Amplified's creators (over 8 million in combined audience) followed by hours of post-pilot interviews.
Q4
How does SPCTR make money?
Two revenue streams. Agency SaaS is the core: a roster-based subscription of $25-60K a year, scaling with roster size. Creator freemium is the second: creators are free as seats under their agency, with a paid tier for individual creators. Software subscriptions at software gross margins, no ad spend, no marketplace take.
Q5
What do the financials look like?
The model is built bottom-up from signed-agency economics, not market-share math. Year 1 is founder-led on this raise: roughly 10 paying agencies and about $300K ARR. Year 2 is the inflection, when a seed round funds a go-to-market team: about 30 agencies and roughly $1.1M ARR. Year 3 reaches a Series A profile: about 75 agencies and roughly $3.2M ARR, at roughly 90% gross margin. A full financial model, detailed use of funds, and term sheet are available on request.

Ben Lahn, solo founder and CEO. A former creator-marketing agency owner who has represented brands in multinational campaigns and built custom tools for brands and creators. Sports psychology background, ten years in medical device product and business development, full-time on SPCTR. The first full-time engineering hire is the top priority of the raise.
Q9
Who is the competition?
A gaming creator agency has three options today, and none is built for it. Brand-side software (CreatorIQ, GRIN, Aspire) is built and priced for the brands that buy creator marketing. An improvised stack of spreadsheets, Notion, and a generic CRM is cheap but not built for rosters, briefs, or media kits. Creator tools (Streamlabs, StreamElements) serve the solo streamer. None of them produce a pricing benchmark for gaming creator deals, because none of them have authorized roster-level performance data. SPCTR is the operating system for the agency-plus-roster middle the other three leave open, and the pricing benchmark the category does not have.
Q10
Why is pricing the moat?
Nobody has built a real price for gaming creator marketing because nobody built the tools to capture the data. Generic creator-marketing software wasn't made for gaming, so the data it produces isn't the right data. The pricing engine is a translation layer between two sides who can't currently talk to each other: creators and agencies who can't prove what their work is worth, and brands outside gaming who don't understand which of the hundreds of games and thousands of niche creators they should care about. SPCTR's wedge is the agency: every signed agency brings its whole roster onto the platform with authorized cross-platform performance data. That data feeds a pricing engine that values each deal on the game, the creator's niche and persona, and the risk of the work. Without the right tools producing the right data, nobody else can do this. Once SPCTR is the price the industry stands on, the category opens to brand spend that currently stays away.

Q12
What are the risks?
SPCTR is early: a solo founder, one signed customer, and a product still being built out. Comparable creator-marketing tools sit at $1,500 to $2,500 per month for less specific products, so the $25-60K price tier is established in the market; SPCTR earning it past Amplified is what the raise tests. The agency-sales motion that turns one customer into many is not yet built. The raise is small and targeted on purpose. It funds testing exactly those things.
