Investor FAQ

The straight answers.

SPCTR is the operating system for gaming creator agencies and their creators. We are raising $400K. Here are the questions investors ask first, answered plainly.

Updated May 22, 2026
Q1
What is SPCTR?
Software that runs a gaming creator agency and its whole roster in one connected workflow. The agency works from a console: the creator roster with live cross-platform analytics, campaigns broken into briefs, brief assignment, and a deal pipeline. Each creator gets a command center: a content calendar and daily briefing, their assigned briefs, cross-platform analytics, an AI Coach, and an auto-built media kit. One system, two connected sides, instead of spreadsheets and group chats.
Q2
Who is it for, and why sell to agencies?
Gaming creator agencies and the creators on their rosters. The agency is the paying customer; creators come onto the platform under their agency. One agency signature puts a whole roster on the platform at once. The agency holds the budget and the roster comes with the deal, which is far more efficient than signing creators one at a time.
Q3
Do you have customers?
Yes. Amplified IM, a mobile-gaming creator agency with a 75-plus creator roster, is signed on a 12-month agreement (auto-renews) and launches on the platform June 15, 2026. As the founding customer, Amplified is on a $1,000/month founding rate, locked for life while the agreement remains in continuous effect, roughly $11K in year one. A future rate change would require a signed addendum from both parties. The creator-facing side of the platform was hardened through a closed pilot with 15 of Amplified's creators (over 8 million in combined audience) followed by hours of post-pilot interviews.
Q4
How does SPCTR make money?
Two revenue streams. Agency SaaS is the core: a roster-based subscription of $25-60K a year, scaling with roster size. Creator freemium is the second: creators are free as seats under their agency, with a paid tier for individual creators. Software subscriptions at software gross margins, no ad spend, no marketplace take.
Q5
What do the financials look like?
The model is built bottom-up from signed-agency economics, not market-share math. Year 1 is founder-led on this raise: roughly 10 paying agencies and about $300K ARR. Year 2 is the inflection, when a seed round funds a go-to-market team: about 30 agencies and roughly $1.1M ARR. Year 3 reaches a Series A profile: about 75 agencies and roughly $3.2M ARR, at roughly 90% gross margin. A full financial model, detailed use of funds, and term sheet are available on request.
Q6
How much are you raising, and on what terms?
$400K on a post-money SAFE at a $4M cap, with a 20% discount and a $50K minimum check. The round is targeting a close by July 17, 2026. SPCTR is the only entity raising, and is converting from a Colorado LLC to a Delaware C-Corp before the round closes, so investors come onto a clean cap table.
Q7
What does the money do?
It funds three things: serving Amplified IM fully and turning it into a reference customer, the first full-time engineering hire, and signing roughly ten agencies at standard pricing. Allocation is roughly 45% engineering, 25% founder and operations, 20% agency sales, 10% legal, C-corp conversion, and reserve. It is sized for about 15 months of runway, enough to reach seed-ready.
Q8
Who is building it?
Ben Lahn, solo founder and CEO. A former creator-marketing agency owner who has represented brands in multinational campaigns and built custom tools for brands and creators. Sports psychology background, ten years in medical device product and business development, full-time on SPCTR. The first full-time engineering hire is the top priority of the raise.
Q9
Who is the competition?
A gaming creator agency has three options today, and none is built for it. Brand-side software (CreatorIQ, GRIN, Aspire) is built and priced for the brands that buy creator marketing. An improvised stack of spreadsheets, Notion, and a generic CRM is cheap but not built for rosters, briefs, or media kits. Creator tools (Streamlabs, StreamElements) serve the solo streamer. SPCTR is the operating system for the agency-plus-roster middle the other three leave open.
Q10
What about NXTE?
NXTE is a commercial design partner. The round funds building out SPCTR's brand-facing side of the platform alongside them. SPCTR owns its data and stays non-exclusive. Any brand-side revenue it produces is upside; it is not in the financial projections.
Q11
What are the risks?
SPCTR is early: a solo founder, one signed customer, and a product still being built out. Standard $25-60K pricing is unproven until the next agencies sign, and the agency-sales motion that turns one customer into many is not yet built. The raise is small and targeted on purpose. It funds testing exactly those things.
